Documentation

Add Hangar

To define a hangar: All measurements should be added using meters and decimals. I.E. 1 for 1 meter, 1.5 for one and a half meters, or 3.05 for 10 feet.

  • Enter the name of the hangar in the Name field.
  • Define the points corresponding to corners of the hangar or ramp space.
  • Click on the Add Field button to define additional points for more complex hangar and ramp spaces.
  • Progress around the edge of the hangar space clockwise to prevent overlapping edges.
  • To the right of the input area is a preview window to allow you to better visualize the entered points.
  • This window can be zoomed in and out using the scroll wheel and panned by left clicking and dragging.
  • Once you are satisfied with your hangar click ‘Submit’ to save.

Timeline

  • The timeline displays the arrival, departure, and movement times for your aircraft and equipment.
  • Click and drag to scroll forwards and backwards in time.
  • Use the scroll wheel or pinch to view larger or smaller time scales.
  • Dates and times are displayed at the bottom of the timeline and a solid red line indicates the current time.

Schedule Tab

  • Aircraft are displayed as blue bars labeled with the N number of that aircraft or the name of a piece of equipment.
  • Click on one of these bars and it will become active and change to a yellow color.
  • Click in the middle of an active selection to move the entire schedule block.
  • Click and drag the left side of an active selection to change the arrival time, or the right side to change the departure time.
  • Click the red ‘X’ next to the active selection to delete that schedule block.
  • Double clicking on a selection will open a menu allowing you to precisely enter new arrival and departure times.

Movement Tab

  • The movement tab shows when aircraft and equipment are moved in the hangar to accommodate changing layouts.
  • When an aircraft or piece of equipment is placed in the schedule, a corresponding entry is made in the movement tab.
  • The movement tab will display a light blue background corresponding to to the arrival and departure times in the schedule tab.
  • Inside the light blue background a darker blue bar represents the times that the aircraft or equipment must be moved.
  • Click the red ‘X’ next to the active selection to delete that schedule block.
  • To initially place the aircraft at the time of its arrival click the dark blue bar.
  • If the aircraft or equipment is moved during its time in the hangar or ramp space additional dark blue bars will appear letting staff know when movements must take place at a glance.

Hangar Menu

Select Hangar

  • This area displays all hangars owned by the user.
  • The active hangar name appears with a check mark as well as the left upper corner of the page.
  • Selecting a hangar will change the active hangar.

View Hangar

  • View hangar allows you to select a date and time to view the hangar 3d model

View Hangar Now

  • View hangar now displays the hangar at the current date and time.

Add Hanagar

  • Selecting this option will open the Add Hangar Window.

Remove Hanagar

  • Selecting this option will open the Remove Hangar Window.
  • Select the desired hangar from the drop down menu and click remove.
  • Warning! Removing a hangar will remove all schedule and positioning information for that hangar.
  • This information is permanently deleted and cannot be recovered.

Action Menu

Schedule Aircraft

  • Add an aircraft from the ‘Actions’ dropdown menu by selecting ‘Schedule Aircraft’.
  • Enter the N number of the aircraft.
  • N-228US will be accepted as a valid N number while N228US will not.
  • Click ‘Search’ and the FAA records of your aircraft will be displayed.
  • Verify that you have the entered the correct aircraft and select the desired arrival and departure times.
  • First click the arrival date and time on the calendar, then the departure date and time.
  • When you are finished, press ‘Apply’ and then ‘Add’ to add the aircraft to the timeline.

Schedule Equipment

  • Add an aircraft from the ‘Actions’ dropdown menu by selecting ‘Schedule Equipment’.
  • Select the desired equipment category.
  • Be aware that this category allows you to select an aircraft option so that aircraft that are not part of the FAA database can be entered into a hangar.
  • Next Select a subcategory and finally a piece of equipment.
  • Verify that you have the entered the correct equipment and select the desired arrival and departure times.
  • First click the arrival date and time on the calendar, then the departure date and time.
  • When you are finished press ‘Apply’ and then ‘Add’ to add the aircraft to the timeline.

Submit a bug report

  • Submit a bug report from the ‘Actions’ dropdown menu by selecting ‘Submit a bug report’. When submitting a bug report please be as descriptive as possible. This allows us to tie your bug report to automated logs in order to find timely solutions to issues.

Log Off

  • Log Off from the ‘Actions’ dropdown menu by selecting ‘Log Off’. Logging of ends your session with HangarHero.com. Any further action will require you to re-enter your password. We highly recommend you log off whenever not actively using the service in order protect your data.

View the 3d Hangar

  • Select ‘View Hangar Now’ to view the hangar at the current time, or select ‘View Hangar’ to choose a time and date to view.

3D Hangar Overview

This window displays the location of all aircraft and equipment scheduled on the hangar floor at a given time.

  • On the left side of the window, a 3D render of the hangar at the selected time is displayed.
  • On the right side of the window, all aircraft and equipment scheduled to be placed are displayed along with their corresponding controls.
  • Underneath the 3D render is a control panel for changing the displayed time as well as a save button to store changed information to the database.

3D Render

  • The 3D render portion of the window can be controlled by both mouse and touchscreen interfaces.
  • To pan around the render, either left click and drag the mouse or swipe the touchscreen in the desired vector of motion.
  • To zoom in on an area of the 3D render, either use the mouse scroll wheel or a pinching action on the touchscreen.
  • To move the center point of the render, right click and drag the mouse.
  • Aircraft and equipment can be repositioned by either left clicking and dragging with the mouse or selecting and dragging with a touchscreen.

Aircraft and Equipment Controls

  • Each piece of aircraft and equipment added in the schedule will appear in this section.
  • If the aircraft or equipment has not yet been added to the hangar, the corresponding controls will consist of a “+” symbol.
  • Clicking the “+” symbol will add a 3D model of the aircraft or equipment to the 3D render.
  • Once an aircraft or equipment has been added, more tools will become available and the control section will highlight when an aircraft or equipment is moused over in the 3D render.
  • The tools that will become available upon adding an aircraft or equipment allow to rotate the 3D model in either a clockwise or counterclockwise direction as well as an option to remove the 3D model from the 3D render.

Time and Save Controls

  • There are two primary ways of changing the time displayed on the 3D render.
  • There is a slider bar immediately under the 3D render window that allows the user to select the time on the currently selected date.
  • If the screen size of the viewing device is too small for exact positioning of this slider bar, it will be replaced by drop down menus allowing for exact time selection.
  • Below the slider bar is the Save button. This button stores the location for all aircraft and equipment to the server.
  • If the Save button is not clicked before leaving the window or changing times, no positional information will be stored to the server.
  • The Save button is flanked by buttons to jump the 3D render window to either the previous or next days.
  • All the positions that were changed has an effect on the relevant aircraft and equipment until the time that they are scheduled to leave that hangar space.
